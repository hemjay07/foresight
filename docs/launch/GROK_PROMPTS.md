# Grok Research Prompts for Influencer Discovery

Copy-paste these into Grok (X's AI) to research potential partners.

---

## 1. Find Fantasy/Gaming Influencers

```
Find Crypto Twitter influencers with 30K-150K followers who have tweeted about fantasy sports, DraftKings, FPL, or crypto gaming in the past 3 months.

Focus on accounts that:
- Have high engagement rates (not bot followers)
- Actually reply to their audience
- Are active in the CT community
- Have promoted crypto projects before (but not scams)

For each, give me:
- Handle
- Follower count
- Why they're relevant
- A recent relevant tweet
```

---

## 2. Find Base Ecosystem Influencers

```
Find Twitter influencers with 30K-150K followers who are active in the Base L2 ecosystem.

Look for people who:
- Have tweeted about Base, Coinbase, or Base-native projects
- Are builders or early adopters, not just airdrop farmers
- Have an engaged, real following

List their handles with recent Base-related tweets.
```

---

## 3. Find Builder/Founder Influencers

```
Find Crypto Twitter accounts with 30K-150K followers run by people who are also builders or founders.

These are influencers who:
- Run their own projects or have in the past
- Understand product development
- Have credibility beyond just content
- Would make good advisors

List handles with what they've built.
```

---

## 4. Analyze a Specific Influencer

```
Analyze @[INSERT HANDLE] for partnership potential:

1. What's their engagement rate? (likes+replies vs followers)
2. Do they reply to their audience or just broadcast?
3. What have they promoted in the past 6 months?
4. Any red flags? (scam promotions, controversy, drama)
5. What topics do they care most about?
6. Who do they interact with frequently?
7. Would their audience be interested in crypto fantasy gaming?
```

---

## 5. Find Fantasy Top Users

```
Who promoted, talked about, or played Fantasy Top (the fantasy crypto influencer game) on Twitter?

Find accounts that:
- Tried or promoted Fantasy Top
- Complained about Fantasy Top (these are potential switchers!)
- Talked about fantasy gaming in crypto context

These are pre-qualified leads who already understand the concept.
```

---

## 6. Check Engagement Quality

```
For @[INSERT HANDLE], analyze their last 20 tweets:

- Average likes and retweets per post?
- Do real accounts reply, or mostly bots/spam?
- What time do they usually post?
- What type of content performs best?
- Do their followers seem real or purchased?
- Overall engagement rate?
```

---

## 7. Find Accessible Mid-Tier Accounts

```
Find Crypto Twitter accounts with 30K-80K followers who:
- Have open DMs or are known to respond to messages
- Are active (posted in last 48 hours)
- Engage with their replies regularly
- Aren't so big that they're inaccessible
- Have promoted projects before without being "ad accounts"

These would be good for partnership outreach.
```

---

## 8. Find Mutual Connections

```
Who are the mutual connections or frequent interactions between these accounts: [LIST 3-5 HANDLES YOU'VE ALREADY IDENTIFIED]

Looking for:
- People they all engage with
- People who engage with all of them
- Potential warm intro opportunities

This helps find clusters of accessible influencers.
```

---

## 9. Competitive Research

```
What crypto gaming or fantasy projects have been promoted on CT in the past 6 months?

For each:
- What was the project?
- Who promoted it?
- How did CT respond? (positive, negative, ignored)
- Is the project still active?

This helps understand what's worked and who's open to gaming promotions.
```

---

## 10. Weekly Batch Research

```
Give me 10 Crypto Twitter accounts (30K-150K followers) that I should reach out to this week for a fantasy sports crypto game partnership.

Criteria:
- High engagement rate (3%+)
- Posted in last 3 days (active)
- Relevant interests (gaming, fantasy, competitions, Base)
- Accessible (reply to people, open DMs likely)
- No red flags

For each, give me a personalized hook I could use in a DM.
```

---

## Pro Tips for Using Grok

1. **Be specific** - The more criteria you give, the better the results
2. **Ask for examples** - "Give me a recent tweet" helps you personalize
3. **Iterate** - If results aren't great, refine your prompt
4. **Verify** - Grok can hallucinate; spot-check the accounts it suggests
5. **Use for analysis** - Best for analyzing specific accounts you've found
