## Why SIGNAL Wins

### The Formula
**Trading Card** (visual concept) + **SIGNAL** (name) + **"I called it."** (statement) = ✅ Human Feel

### What the user FEELS
- Smart (you predicted it)
- Confident (no luck involved)
- Proud (shareable without being obnoxious)
- Insider (only traders understand "the call")

### What makes it different
- Not a stat dump (Moonberg lesson)
- Not generic (has personality/voice)
- Not trying too hard (simple, confident)
- Culturally native (CT traders get it immediately)

### Key Numbers

| Element | Size | Font | Color | Notes |
|---------|------|------|-------|-------|
| "⭐ SIGNAL" | 32px | Inter 600 | #F59E0B | Top-left anchor |
| "I CALLED IT" | 56px | Plus Jakarta Sans 700 | #FAFAFA | Hero statement (BIGGEST) |
| Rank/Points | 18px | JetBrains Mono 500 | #A1A1AA | Supporting details |
| Avatar | 200x200px | — | — | Circle, centered, holographic shine |

### Visual Effects

1. **Holographic shine** — 45° linear gradient on avatar (subtle, premium feel)
2. **Glow** — Radial gradient behind avatar (#F59E0B @ 30% opacity)
3. **Dividers** — Dashed lines above/below "I CALLED IT" (subtle separation)
4. **Rank badge** — Optional small gold circle in top-right corner

## Why This Beats Moonberg's Standard

| Criterion | Moonberg | Our Card |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Single strong idea** | One terminal metaphor | Insider who predicted the market |
| **Bold copy** | "ONE TERMINAL..." | "I CALLED IT" |
| **Typographic hierarchy** | Massive text + tiny text | 56px statement + 18px stats |
| **Visual tension** | Image vs. text, dark vs. light | Avatar glow + dashed dividers |
| **Has a voice** | Confident, unapologetic | Personal victory, insider knowledge |
| **Memorable** | Sticks with you | "I called it" = pure confidence |

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Avatar loads slowly | Medium | Pre-cache + fallback to tier badge |
| "I called it" feels arrogant | Low | Context (rank number) softens it |
| Card doesn't fit OG image specs | Low | Tested 1200x630 (standard) |
| Mobile rendering is ugly | Medium | Test early on actual devices |
| Users don't understand "SIGNAL" | Low | On-page tooltip explains concept |
